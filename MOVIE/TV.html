TV SERIES

GUILTY CROWN (2011)


Directed by: Shinpei Ezaki

Studio: Production I.G

Genre: Sci-Fi, Super Power, Action

Guilty Crown is set in Japan of the year 2039 and revolves around Shu Ouma, a boy who has acquired an ability called the "Power of the Kings" that allows him to draw out tools or weapons from another person. Shu joins a resistance group named "Funeral Parlor" which aims to restore Japan's independence from an international organization known as the GHQ.



NEON GENESIS EVANGELION (1995)


Directed by: Hideaki Anno

Studio: Gainax and Tatsunoko Production

Genre: Apocalyptic, Mecha, Psychological drama

Neon Genesis Evangelion is set fifteen years after a worldwide cataclysm, particularly in the futuristic fortified city of Tokyo-3. Shinji, a teenage boy who was recruited by his father Gendo to the Nerv organization to pilot a bio-machine mecha named "Evangelion" into combat against beings called "Angels". The series explores the experiences and emotions of Evangelion pilots and members of Nerv as they try to prevent Angels from causing more cataclysms. While the initial episodes focus on mecha fighting but the later episodes tend to go deeper into the psyches of the characters, the series begins to question the reality and the existence. Neon Genesis Evangelion was based on the Hideaki Anno's own clinical depression.
