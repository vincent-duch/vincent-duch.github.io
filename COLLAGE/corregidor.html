CORREGIDOR, PHILIPPINES

Corregidor is an island in the entrance of Manila Bay in the Philippines which serves as the countryâ€™s immediate harbor defense against enemy warships during the World War II. After the War, the island serves as a historical monument that attracts the tourist to take a look at the ruins of military installations, wrecked cannons and artillery, and war-ravaged buildings.



HOW TO GET THERE

Since the island is currently owned by Sun Cruises, here is the recommended option to visit the Corregidor Island.

-Ride LRT 1 from Roosevelt (if North) or Baclaran (if South) to Gil Puyat Avenue.

-Book a ferry from Sun Cruise Terminal in Esplanade Seaside Terminal, Pasay City.

-Upon arriving at the ferry, the ferry will drop you to the island.
